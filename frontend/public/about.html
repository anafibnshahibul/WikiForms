WikiForms

About WikiForms

A free, open-source form and quiz builder for the Wikimedia ecosystem.

WikiForms was created to give the Wikimedia community a privacy-respecting, open-source alternative to third-party form builders — one that lives entirely within the Wikimedia ecosystem.

What is WikiForms?

WikiForms is a drag-and-drop form and quiz builder hosted on Wikimedia Toolforge. It allows Wikipedia editors, researchers, and community members to create structured data collection forms and secure quizzes — without leaving the Wikimedia ecosystem and without sharing data with third-party corporations.

Login is handled entirely through your existing Wikipedia account via MediaWiki OAuth 2.0 — no separate sign-up required.

Features

🔐 Wikipedia Login

One-click login with your existing Wikipedia account. No passwords stored.

📋 Drag & Drop Builder

Build forms with multiple question types, sections, and custom options.

🌐 Multilingual

Interface available in English, Bengali, Spanish, French — and growing.

🔒 Encrypted Storage

Form questions are encrypted at rest using AES-256-CBC.

👥 Collaborators

Share edit and view access with other Wikipedia users.

⏱ Scheduled Quizzes

Set automatic start and end times for time-limited quizzes.

🔗 Wiki Links

Rich text editor with native Wikipedia interwiki link support.

🌍 Contribute

Any Wikipedia user can contribute translations to new languages.

Why WikiForms?

When Wikipedia community members need to collect data — for surveys, admin tasks, research, or quizzes — they typically rely on Google Forms or similar third-party tools. This means community data leaves the Wikimedia ecosystem and is subject to corporate privacy policies.

WikiForms keeps everything within Wikimedia infrastructure, is fully open-source, and respects the community's values of transparency and privacy.

Technology

  • Backend: Laravel (PHP) on Wikimedia Toolforge
  • Frontend: React + Vite
  • Auth: MediaWiki OAuth 2.0
  • Database: MySQL/MariaDB (Toolforge)
